ğŸš‡ Transit Options

Charlotte has limited but growing transit infrastructure:

  • LYNX Blue Line: Light rail running from University City (north) through Uptown to Pineville (south). Key station for in-town neighborhoods: 7th Street (Elizabeth/NoDa adjacent). NoDa, Wilmore, and South End neighborhoods have Blue Line access.
  • LYNX Gold Line: Streetcar running through Uptown. 7th Street station provides Elizabeth and NoDa access to Uptown. Limited but useful for specific commute patterns.
  • CATSBus: Bus network connects most neighborhoods to Uptown. Less time-competitive than biking for most close-in neighborhoods.
  • Uptown CycleLink: Protected bike lane network within Uptown connecting the Little Sugar Creek Greenway at 7th Street to the Uptown core. This is the infrastructure that makes greenway bike commuting practical for several of the target neighborhoods.

Bottom line on transit: For his Uptown commute, biking is better than transit from all close-in target neighborhoods. Transit becomes relevant only for days when biking is not feasible (heavy rain, illness, post-work obligations). The Blue Line is useful for NoDa and Wilmore residents specifically.
