üè´ How Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools (CMS) Works

The Assignment System

CMS uses a neighborhood assignment model: every student is assigned to a "home school" based on their residential address. This is your default/fallback school. However, CMS has an extensive school choice system on top of this:

  • Neighborhood school: Automatic based on address. Free busing within zone.
  • Magnet schools: Theme-based programs (Montessori, STEAM, Arts, IB, World Languages) open to all CMS students via annual lottery. Up to 3 choices.
  • Charter schools: Publicly funded but independently operated. Separate lottery. No home address requirement.
  • Private schools: No assignment system; apply directly.

The Magnet Lottery

Application window: mid-October through early December for the following school year. Results announced early January. You can select up to 3 school choices. If not selected, placed on a waitlist (seats often open through August as families decline).

Transportation note: Bus service to a magnet school is only provided if it falls within your designated transportation zone (determined by your assigned high school). Out-of-zone magnet choices are allowed but may require you to provide transportation.

CMS Magnet Overhaul Coming: CMS is proposing to reduce magnet themes from 16 to 6 (consolidating around Arts, Montessori, STEM, Early College, World Languages, and IB/LI/TD). Vote expected 2026; changes not before 2027-28 at earliest. This creates some uncertainty but potentially simplifies choices by the time your kids enter Kindergarten.

Pre-K in CMS ‚Äî Honest Assessment

CMS runs Bright Beginnings Pre-K (63 schools, ~3,240 four-year-olds). Eligibility is needs-based and income-based. At $280k household income, your family almost certainly does not qualify. North Carolina Pre-K (state-funded) has the same income restrictions. CMS Bright Beginnings is not a realistic option for this family.

Your Pre-K path: MECK Pre-K (free, no income limit ‚Äî see below), private school Pre-K (Charlotte Prep, Charlotte Country Day, Charlotte Latin), or a local independent Pre-K program for a bridge year while you navigate the 2027-28 Kindergarten lottery and admissions cycle.
