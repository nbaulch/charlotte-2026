Myers Park is Charlotte's most prestigious residential neighborhood ‚Äî a 1910s streetcar suburb designed with grand boulevards, towering oak canopies, Colonial Revival and Tudor Revival architecture. Queens Road West is its signature street: a divided boulevard with a median of mature trees and a parade of large historic homes.

This is Charlotte's equivalent of Minneapolis's Kenwood or Lowry Hill ‚Äî established, beautiful, architecturally significant, and quite affluent. Median household income in 28207 is ~$230,000. The community skews conservative by Charlotte standards (Niche notes "residents tend to lean conservative"), is overwhelmingly white (91%), and carries an old-money, established reputation. Not pretentious in a nouveau-riche way ‚Äî more old Charlotte families who've been here for generations.

For this family from Lynnhurst, the architecture and aesthetics are appealing, but the social culture may feel different: Myers Park is more conservative, more formal, and has a certain established-wealth gravitas that Lynnhurst doesn't. You would be entering as younger transplants into an expensive neighborhood. Worth assessing in person.

31% of households have children ‚Äî the highest family density of any neighborhood on this list. Families with young kids are a core demographic here.

CLT flight path ‚Äî significant risk

Myers Park was specifically named in Charlotte news coverage of the proposed 2024 CLT flight path expansion. The City Council approved studying new departure routes (tripling from 8 to 27 paths), pending FAA approval. Myers Park, not currently affected, could see new aircraft noise if approved. Timeline: 1+ year for FAA review. Monitor closely.
